Did you know that many famous female journalists were Girl Scouts?! Including Katie Couric, Gayle King, Lisa Ling, Gloria Steinem, Taylor Bryant,

Throughout history women

have been taking a stand an raising their voices against important issues. Before we move ahead to the present and future, we must get inspiration from the past.

Many of the journalists you may research helped move the women’s rights movement forward by challenging boundaries in their writing.

After researching one journalist, create a picture, song, poem, or something else of your choosing to tell the story of her, or a part of her story that you can connect with.

Savannah Guthrie, Nikole Hannah-Jones, Jo Ling Kent, Nina Parker, and Kate Snow.


Gloria Steinem

Gloria Steinem is a feminist who rose with the Grassroots movement. She began her career as a journalist in New York in a time where her ideas were turned down. She was a huge part of the feminist and Equal Rights movement. Steinem founded the Ms. magazine, wrote several books, and received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from Barack Obama.


Nikole Hannah-Jones

Nikole Hannah-Jones is a Pulitzer

Prize-winning investigative journalist. She is known for her reporting of civil rights. A New York Times reporter, in 2019 she launched the 1916 project, to commemorate the 400th anniversary of the beginning of slavery. She also helped to create the Ida B. Wells Society for Investigative Reporting.
